Fufu

Description

Fufu with soup is a dish from West Africa which is predominantly eaten by The Asante people, though many people enjoy this delicacy too. It can be eaten with any kind of soup depending on individual preference. It is estimated that, at least every household in the Asante Kingdom eats fufu everyday. It is very nutritious which contains all the necessary nutrients.

Preparation of this food differs across the various countries in West Africa as well as the individual tribe/ethnic in that particular country. The picture above is fufu and light soup from Ghana in particular, the Asante tribe. Ingredients for preparation also differs depending on your tribe. Other tribes use cassava, yam, potato or cocoyam as the main food source in preparation. We will focus on the Asante people and their preparation.

Ingredients:

  • Cassava
  • Plantain / Cocoyam
  • Meat
  • Fish
  • vegetables
  • Spices

Steps/Preparation:

  1. Cassava and Plantain are boiled together
  2. Soup preparation starts whiles the cassava and plantain is on fire.
  3. Whiles the soup is on fire, you start to pound the cassava and plantain together using mortar and pestle.
  4. Serving of this dish depends on how individual wants to eat it either hot or warm.
